Weighted ATS Scorer
Rule-based ML — Composite Scoring
Six-component weighted scoring: Keyword Match (30%), Skills Match (20%), Experience Relevance (20%), Formatting (10%), Readability (10%), Section Completeness (10%). Final 0–100 score = Σ(weight × component).
Formula
ATS = Σ(wᵢ × sᵢ) × 100

Flesch-Kincaid Readability
Statistical NLP
Flesch Reading Ease score measures resume language clarity. Optimal range 60–80 for professional resumes. Penalizes overly complex (jargon-heavy) or overly simple text. Part of 10% readability ATS component.
Library

Rule-based Ranking
Ranks missing skills by frequency in JD using regex count. Most-mentioned missing skills ranked highest — e.g., if "Kubernetes" appears 4 times in JD and missing from resume, it's Priority 1 to add.

ATS Scoring Formula
6-Component Weighted ATS Score
ATS_Score = Σ(wᵢ × sᵢ) × 100
w₁=0.30 × keyword_match ← TF-IDF: resume∩JD keywords / JD keywords
w₂=0.20 × skills_match ← exact + fuzzy skill overlap
w₃=0.20 × exp_relevance ← TF-IDF cosine(experience_text, JD)
w₄=0.10 × formatting ← contact info + length + bullets + dates
w₅=0.10 × readability ← Flesch-Kincaid ease score (optimal 60–80)
w₆=0.10 × section_complete ← found_sections / 6 expected sections

IRT 3-Parameter Logistic
Psychometric Model
P(correct|θ) = c + (1-c) / (1+e^(-a(θ-b))). Parameters: θ=ability, b=difficulty, a=discrimination, c=0.25 guessing. S-curve maps ability to answer probability. Core of the adaptive engine.
NumPySciPyθ range: -3 to +3
📐
MLE Newton-Raphson
θ Update Algorithm
After each answer, maximizes log-likelihood L(θ) = Σ[Xᵢlog(Pᵢ) + (1-Xᵢ)log(1-Pᵢ)]. Newton-Raphson iterates: θ_new = θ + L'(θ)/L''(θ). Converges in 3–5 iterations per update.
SciPy optimizeConverges in ~5 iters
🎯
Maximum Fisher Information
Question Selection
I(θ) = a² × [(P-c)² / ((1-c)²P(1-P))]. Selects question with highest I(θ) at current θ from question bank. Guarantees each question maximizes information gain — minimizes test length (CAT principle).
NumPy argmax(I)CAT algorithm
🔄
TF-IDF Anti-Repetition
Deduplication Filter
Before serving each question, cosine similarity checks against all questions shown in current session. Rejects similarity > 0.7. Prevents near-duplicate question exposure and ensures variety across topics.
scikit-learnthreshold 0.7

Detects if answer follows Situation-Task-Action-Result framework. Regex + keyword rules identify each component: "when I was at…" (S), "I had to…" (T), "I decided to…" (A), "which resulted in…" (R). Scores 0–4 STAR components found.

Filler Word Detector
NLP — Speech Quality
Counts filler words: um, uh, like, you know, basically, literally, right?, okay so. Regex scan of STT transcript. Filler rate = fillers / total words × 100%. >15% reduces communication score. Common interview red flag.

Optimal HR answer length: 120–180 words (60–90 seconds). Too short (<60 words) → insufficient detail. Too long (>300 words) → unfocused. Word count + NLTK sentence tokenizer measures structure and depth.

Communication Score Formula
HR Round Composite Score
HR_Score = (STAR×30 + Sentiment×20 + Relevance×20 + Grammar×15 + Filler×15) / 100

MediaPipe FaceMesh
Computer Vision — 468 Landmarks
Detects 468 3D facial landmarks in real-time at 30fps. Tracks eye corners, nose tip, lip corners for: gaze direction, head pose (pitch/yaw/roll), eye contact %, smile detection, micro-expressions. Runs browser-side via TensorFlow.js.

Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR)
CV Algorithm — Blink/Gaze
EAR = (‖p2-p6‖ + ‖p3-p5‖) / (2×‖p1-p4‖) using 6 eye landmark points. EAR < 0.2 = blink detected. Blink rate tracking: normal (12-20/min), too high (anxiety) or too low (staring = discomfort). Gaze direction from iris position.
EAR formulaBlink rate trackingGaze direction

Weighted ensemble: eye_contact(30%) + emotion_stability(25%) + posture(25%) + smile_freq(10%) + blink_norm(10%). Polynomial regression normalizes raw CV scores to 0–100 index. Trained on 500 labeled interview recordings.

Confidence Index Formula
Composite Confidence Score (0–100)
CI = 0.30×eye_contact + 0.25×emotion_stability + 0.25×posture + 0.10×smile_freq + 0.10×blink_norm
eye_contact = frames_looking_at_camera / total_frames × 100
emotion_stability = 100 − (variance_in_emotion × 10) ← lower variance = more stable
posture = shoulder_sym × 40 + lean_score × 40 + arm_open × 20
blink_norm = 100 − |blink_rate − 16| × 5 ← optimal 12–20 blinks/min
